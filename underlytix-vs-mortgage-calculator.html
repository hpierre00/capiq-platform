FAQ

Common questions

Why isn't a mortgage calculator enough for investment properties? ▾
Mortgage calculators are designed for primary residence purchases where the key question is "can I afford this payment?" Investment property financing — especially DSCR loans, hard money, and bridge financing — uses entirely different criteria. Lenders evaluate the deal's income potential and debt coverage, not just your ability to make a payment. A calculator that shows an affordable payment says nothing about whether a lender will actually fund the deal.
What is DSCR and why does it matter? ▾
DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio — the ratio of a property's annual rental income to its annual debt payments. Most DSCR lenders require a minimum of 1.0 to 1.25. A deal with a DSCR below this threshold will be declined regardless of your personal credit score. Mortgage calculators don't calculate DSCR. Underlytix does it automatically.
Does Underlytix work for fix-and-flip deals, not just rentals? ▾
Yes. Underlytix analyzes buy-and-hold rental deals, fix-and-flip acquisitions, multifamily purchases, and mixed-use properties. Each deal type uses different scoring criteria — ARV, rehab budget, and projected exit value for flips; DSCR and cash flow for rentals.
